Contextual Inquiry

In order to better understand our users and their plights
we conducted research through observations and questions.
Below are our findings and observations

Reservation Page

When users navigate to "My Events" once logged into RIT Services, they are able to view reservations. This shows the kind of rooms reserved and for what date. The crucial information missing here is the time the room is booked for. Users being unable to view their selected tim reservation can cause a lot of confusion.

Time Selection

In this screen Users are selecting what time they would like their reservation for. On the left side of this picture you can see that users are seeing the time in a calendar mode and in military time instead of standard time This is something else that can cause confusion as most users are used to viewing time in standard form.

Room Selection Page

In this view, users are prompted to select a room based on their chosen time. This is the view of what rooms are or aren't available at the selected time. This view is not at all clear to users on how to choose a room.

Field Observations

User A Demographic:
5th year RIT web and mobile computing major student
24 years old
Used the library Study Room once a week in the past semester
Observer: Josh Samuel
Setting: Golisano Computer Lab
Date & Time: 9/30/25 1:20pm-2:00pm
Device: Laptop
Task: Reserve an individual study room at RIT library



User B Demographic:
Program: Graduate HCI
Year: 1
Booking Frequency: Never Booked, User New to RIT Study & Booking System
Device: Laptop
Task: Booking and reservation an individual study room at RIT library
Setting: Global Village
Date & Time: 30/09/25 @ 4.00pm- 4:25pm

Observation User A

  • Typed RIT library student room on address bar
  • Finger-pointed the description under “Building & Room Information” link in search page results
  • Scrolled down website until user observed “Study Rooms”section

  • Read through and fingering point passage through “Study Rooms”,
    “Classrooms Used as Meeting Rooms”, then stopped at “Room Access & Reservation” Clicked “reserve.rit.edu” link

  • Talked to himself “I want study room this Thursday”

  • Clicked “Add/Remove” link to filter the location for Wallace Library

  • Finger pointing at “add” icons to reserve room he desired then selected it

  • Redirect to the “Reservation Details” website then click the “Create Reservation” green button bottom right to confirm the reservation

    • Post-Observation Interview Questions User A

    1. Observer: “How was the experience”
      User A: It went smoothly
    2. Observer: Have you done this before?
      User A: Yes, a couple times
    3. Observer: How was your experience when you did this for the first time?
      User A: For the first time, there was some confusion and it was not intuitive. It does not display the full building name for the study room because there are study rooms that can be reserved in different buildings other than the library. After selecting the room, it does not notify me that the study room has been reserved. I noticed the “Next Step” button and assumed that it is a confirmation page. I want to add that reserving a room in mobile won’t work because you won’t login in through a browser. Only desktop or laptop works.


    Pain Points
  • Frustrated that reserving room in mobile won’t work

  • Extra unnecessary steps to confirm reservation

  • Confusion with information being displayed for room location and reserve confirmation

  • Although the full building name is displayed in gray horizontal bar, it's not well visible to the user
